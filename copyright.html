1. How to File a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you are the copyright owner (or authorized to act on behalf of one) and believe that material hosted on Unblocked Dev infringes your copyright, please submit a DMCA takedown notice that includes the following information:

  • Identification of the copyrighted work: Describe the copyrighted work that is allegedly infringed, including its location (e.g., a URL or copy of the material).
  • Identification of the infringing content: Clearly identify the specific content you believe infringes your copyright, with enough detail to help us locate it on our game or website.
  • Your contact details: Provide your full name, mailing address, phone number, and email address for communication.
  • Statement of good faith: A statement confirming that you believe in good faith that the material was used without authorization from the copyright holder, their agent, or the law.
  • Statement of accuracy: A statement affirming that the information in your notice is correct and, under penalty of perjury, that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on their behalf.
  • Your signature: Include your physical or electronic signature.

2. Counter-Notification Process

If you believe that the material you posted on Unblocked Dev was wrongly removed or disabled due to a DMCA notice, you may file a counter-notification. A valid counter-notification must include the following:

  • Identification of removed material: Identify the material that was removed, and provide details about where it was located before removal.
  • Statement under penalty of perjury: A statement confirming that you believe the material was removed due to a mistake or misidentification.
  • Consent to jurisdiction: A statement agreeing to the jurisdiction of the federal court in your district (or where your address is located) and accepting service of process from the party who issued the original DMCA notice.
  • Your contact details: Include your name, address, phone number, and email address.
  • Your signature: Provide your physical or electronic signature.
